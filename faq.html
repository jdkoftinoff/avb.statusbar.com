What is AVB?

Audio Video Bridging is a term for a collection of IEEE Standards for time sensitive networking which facilitates the transport of high performance audio and video on a LAN:

  1. IEEE Std. 802.1BA-2011, for profiles and overview.
  2. IEEE Std. 802.1Q-2011, for traffic shaping (FQTSS) and the stream reservation protocol (SRP).
  3. IEEE Std. 802.1AS-2011, for time synchronization (gPTP).
  4. IEEE Std. 1722-2011, for the Audio Video Transport Protocol (AVTP).
  5. IEEE P1722.1-D23, for the Audio Video Discovery, Enumeration, Connection management, and Control protocol (AVDECC)

IEEE Std 1722-2011 builds on the following standards for media packetization:

  1. IEC 61883-6, for the transport of Audio, SMPTE Time Code, and MIDI.
  2. IEC 61883-8, for the transport of Video
  3. 1394 Trade Association document 1999024, which details SMPTE Time Code encapsulation within IEC 61883-6
  4. MIDI MMA AVB Payload Formats
